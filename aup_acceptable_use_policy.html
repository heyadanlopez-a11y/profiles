Emails Which Require Escalation

This section explains when it is necessary to involve the security team regarding a suspicious email. While waiting for security team to engage, Kirkhill team should begin basic email investigation to gather more information (see “Email Investigation” section).

If you respond ‘yes’ to any of the following, contact the security team and begin immediate ‘Hunt and Kill’ procedure:

  1. Suspicious email received by more than one employee.
  2. Message contains a known threat.

If you respond ‘yes’ to any of the following, contact the security team for guidance:

  1. Did the user click or open a link or attachment which appears suspicious or risky?
  2. Does the email appear expertly-crafted, or involve “inside information” such as:
    • Refer to actual current discussions/threads?
    • Include convincing and accurate signature blocks?
    • Refer to valid Kirkhill customers, orders, vendors, part numbers, etc.?
    • Utilize proper logos or company branding of Kirkhill or a Kirkhill contact?
  3. Did the sender seem to know the victim’s job role and exactly the kind of request which this employee would normally handle?
  4. Did the sender spoof or impersonate a Kirkhill employee’s internal @kirkhill.com address even though the message appears to come from someone outside the organization?
  5. Did the sender impersonate a valid, high-ranking employee in an attempt to trick a lower-level employee into divulging information or performing a sensitive transaction? (whale-phishing)
  6. Did the email execute some kind of suspicious action or command without the user having to click on any link or attachment?
  7. Did the email contain some suspicious or dangerous link or attachment which appears to have been received by more than one Kirkhill employee?
  8. Did the employee notice any strange messages or behavior around the time that they opened the email message, link, or attachment?
  9. Did any other employees report suspicious emails which appear to have similar tactics or appearance?

Email Investigation

Emails which appear particularly dangerous or risky should be investigated. Use Defender ‘Explorer’ to try and answer these questions:

  1. Are there more emails in the organization connected to this same message?
  2. Other emails from the same email address?
  3. Other emails from the same IP address?
  4. Other emails with the same subject line (different sender)?
  5. Does Defender ‘Investigations’ show other emails related to this message?
